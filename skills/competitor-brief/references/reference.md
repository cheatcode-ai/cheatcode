# Competitor Brief Reference

## Source Ranking

1. Company-controlled primary sources: homepage, pricing, docs, changelog, status page, security page.
2. Customer evidence: case studies, app marketplaces, review sites, public customer stories.
3. Market evidence: funding announcements, hiring pages, public filings, partner listings.
4. Weak signals: social posts, forum comments, SEO pages, unsourced listicles.

## Brief Structure

Use this order unless the user asks for a custom format:

1. One-line summary.
2. Product and core workflow.
3. ICP and positioning.
4. Pricing and packaging.
5. Traction signals.
6. Competitive comparison.
7. SWOT.
8. Actionable takeaways.

## Risk Rubric

| Risk | Signal |
|---|---|
| Feature parity | Direct overlap in core workflow or buyer promise |
| Distribution | Strong SEO, partner ecosystem, community, marketplace, or sales hiring |
| Pricing pressure | Transparent lower-cost tier or usage-based wedge |
| Trust advantage | Security/compliance claims the user's product lacks |
| Momentum | Frequent releases, funding, hiring, customer logos, integrations |

Mark uncertain observations as inference and state the evidence used.
